Regular cleaning cannot fix this. We remove bonded mineral deposits — safely, without damaging glass or frames.

1

Hard Water Bonds to Glass

Calcium and silica chemically bond to glass over time. Invisible at first, severe after months of exposure.

Cleaning Cannot Remove It

Water, detergent, and repeated scrubbing cannot break chemical bonds. This is not a cleaning problem.

High pressure drives minerals deeper into glass micro-pores, accelerating and spreading the damage.

Wrong Chemicals = Permanent Damage

Uncontrolled harsh acids etch glass irreversibly. One wrong treatment can permanently destroy the surface.

Full mineral removal in a single pass — glass restored to original optical clarity

The mineral layer was still semi-transparent — visible as white streaking against light but glass remained see-through. This confirmed the calcium carbonate and silica deposits had not yet penetrated deep into the glass micro-pores, making it an ideal candidate for early-stage restoration. A single controlled-dwell pass dissolved the surface-bonded mineral layer completely. Had this facade been left untreated for another 6–12 months, the deposits would have progressed to Stage 2 deep bonding — requiring multi-pass treatment at significantly higher cost. This is exactly why early intervention matters.
